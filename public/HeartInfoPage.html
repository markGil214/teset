Common Heart Diseases & Conditions

Understanding heart diseases can help you recognize symptoms early and take preventive measures. Here are some of the most common cardiovascular conditions:

üî• Coronary Artery Disease (CAD)

The most common type of heart disease. Occurs when coronary arteries become narrowed or blocked by cholesterol deposits, reducing blood flow to the heart muscle.

Symptoms:

Chest pain, shortness of breath, fatigue, irregular heartbeat

üíî Heart Attack (Myocardial Infarction)

Occurs when blood flow to part of the heart is blocked for a long enough time that part of the heart muscle is damaged or dies.

Symptoms:

Severe chest pain, sweating, nausea, shortness of breath, arm pain

‚ö° Arrhythmia

Irregular heartbeat that can be too fast, too slow, or irregular. Can affect the heart's ability to pump blood effectively.

Symptoms:

Palpitations, dizziness, fainting, chest discomfort

üí® Heart Failure

Condition where the heart cannot pump blood as well as it should. The heart keeps working but not as efficiently as a healthy heart.

Symptoms:

Shortness of breath, fatigue, swelling in legs/feet/ankles

üö™ Heart Valve Disease

Occurs when one or more heart valves don't work properly, affecting blood flow through the heart chambers.

Symptoms:

Fatigue, shortness of breath, irregular heartbeat, chest pain

ü©∏ High Blood Pressure

Often called the "silent killer" because it usually has no symptoms but can lead to serious heart problems over time.

Symptoms:

Often no symptoms, sometimes headaches, shortness of breath

üö® Emergency Warning Signs - Call 911 Immediately

  • Chest pain or pressure lasting more than a few minutes
  • Pain spreading to arms, neck, jaw, or back
  • Shortness of breath with or without chest discomfort
  • Breaking out in cold sweat
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Light-headedness or sudden dizziness
  • Sudden severe headache
  • Sudden loss of vision or speech

Heart-Healthy Diet Guidelines

  • Eat more: Fruits, vegetables, whole grains, fish, nuts, legumes
  • Choose: Lean meats, low-fat dairy products, olive oil
  • Limit: Saturated fats, trans fats, sodium, added sugars
  • Avoid: Processed foods, excessive alcohol, large portions

Risk Factors for Heart Disease

üî¥ Modifiable Risk Factors (You Can Control)

  • High blood pressure
  • High cholesterol levels
  • Smoking and tobacco use
  • Physical inactivity
  • Obesity
  • Diabetes
  • Unhealthy diet
  • Excessive alcohol consumption
  • Stress

üîµ Non-Modifiable Risk Factors (Cannot Be Changed)

  • Age (risk increases with age)
  • Gender (men have higher risk at younger ages)
  • Family history of heart disease
  • Race/ethnicity
  • Previous heart attack or stroke

When to See a Healthcare Provider

Regular check-ups are important, but you should see a doctor immediately if you experience:

üè• Immediate Medical Attention

  • Chest pain or pressure
  • Severe shortness of breath
  • Fainting or near-fainting
  • Rapid or irregular heartbeat
  • Severe fatigue
